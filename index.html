2. Priority Decision Tree — Which Email Wins

When a customer is eligible for multiple flows simultaneously, this tree determines which track takes priority. Read top-to-bottom — the first match wins.

Core rule: The highest-intent behavioral signal always takes priority. Checkout > Cart > Browse > Welcome > Campaigns. A new subscriber who starts checkout gets checkout emails, not welcome emails.
  • 🔔 Customer event detected (any Shopify event or new subscription)
    • Placed Order in last 20 days? → YES
      🟣 POST-PURCHASE TRACK
      • Send: Thank You email only (order confirmation + care preview)
      • Suppress: ALL other flows and campaigns for 20 days
      • After 20 days: Profile re-enters normal eligibility based on current behavior
    • NO purchase → Started Checkout in last 7 days? → YES
      • Also has Added-to-Cart items? → YES
        🔴🟠 DUAL TRACK — Checkout + Cart on offset days
        • Checkout emails get priority days (Day 0, 1, 3, 7)
        • Cart digest emails get offset days (Day 2, 5)
        • Never two emails on the same day
        • Suppress: Browse Abandonment
        • See Section 6: Dual-Track Scenario
      • No cart items → 🔴 CHECKOUT TRACK (Solo)
        • Full checkout recovery sequence: Flow emails → Digest follow-ups
        • Suppress: Browse Abandonment, Cart Digest
    • NO checkout → Added to Cart in last 21 days? → YES
      🟠 CART TRACK
      • Cart recovery sequence: Flow email → Cart Digest follow-ups
      • Suppress: Browse Abandonment
      • If customer later starts checkout → escalate to Checkout Track (see Intent Escalation)
    • NO cart → Viewed Product in last 30 days? → YES
      🔵 BROWSE ABANDONMENT TRACK
      • Browse recovery sequence: 4 emails over 14 days
      • If customer adds to cart → exit browse, enter Cart Track
      • If customer starts checkout → exit browse, enter Checkout Track
    • NO browse → New Subscriber (under 21 days)? → YES
      🟢 WELCOME TRACK
      • Currently: 1 welcome email (immediate)
      • Does NOT suppress any other flow — if subscriber browses/carts/checks out, those flows fire in parallel
      • Momentum preserved: Welcome + Checkout can co-exist
    • NOT new → ⚪ LIFECYCLE — Campaigns only
      • Regular campaigns: New arrivals (2×/mo), Editorial (2×/mo), Collection spotlight (1×/mo)
      • Seasonal campaigns per calendar

6. Dual-Track Scenario: Checkout + Cart Items

When a customer has both Started Checkout items and separate Added to Cart items (items they browsed/carted but didn't proceed to checkout with), both tracks run with interleaved timing. Checkout gets priority days.

Interleave rule: Never send two emails on the same day for the same profile. Checkout emails claim odd/key days, cart fills the gaps. Maximum: 1 email per day, 4 per week during the dual-track window.
DayTrackEmailContentStatus
+1 hour CHECKOUT Checkout Flow #1 "Complete your purchase" — checkout items with images, prices. Immediate urgency (warm, not aggressive). LIVE
+24h (Day 1) CHECKOUT Checkout Flow #2 Trust & reassurance — returns, warranty, financing. Checkout items reminder. LIVE
Day 2 CART (offset) Cart Digest #1 ALL cart items (not in checkout) consolidated. "While you're deciding, you also had your eye on these…" Styling tips. NEW
Day 3 CHECKOUT Checkout Digest Consolidated checkout items + social proof. Reviews. "Customers who bought these also loved…" NEW
Day 5 CART (offset) Cart Digest #2 Cart items + social proof + complementary suggestions. Secondary conversion opportunity. NEW
Day 7 CHECKOUT Checkout Final "Your pieces are waiting." Service offer (engraving, resizing). Final gentle close for checkout items. REC

Result: 6 emails over 7 days. 1 immediate + 5 over the week = roughly 5/week during peak recovery. Acceptable for high-intent dual-track customers with $750+ AOV. The customer has shown intent on multiple items — this level of attention is warranted.

10. Re-Entry & Cooldown Rules

TrackCycle Ends WhenCooldown PeriodRe-Entry TriggerOverride
Checkout Purchase OR 7 days with no action 7 days after last checkout email New Checkout Started event New checkout event during cooldown → reset immediately, re-enter
Cart Purchase, checkout, OR 10 days with no action 7 days after last cart email New Added to Cart event New cart event during cooldown → reset immediately, re-enter
Browse Cart add, checkout, purchase, OR 14 days None — new views can retrigger New Viewed Product event Frequency cap: max 1 browse email per 48h
Post-Purchase 20 days after purchase 20 days (the suppression IS the cooldown) Automatic — suppression lifts, profile re-evaluates No override. 20-day suppression is absolute.
Cooldown rationale for fine jewelry ($750 AOV, 27-day cycle):
  • 7-day cooldown = ~25% of the average sales cycle. Long enough to avoid fatigue, short enough to catch renewed intent.
  • New intent event overrides cooldown because renewed action signals genuine interest. A customer who adds to cart 3 days into cooldown is actively shopping — respond immediately.
  • Hard limit: No more than 3 complete checkout recovery cycles per 90-day period. After the 3rd full cycle for the same profile, suppress for 30 days. This prevents "professional browsers" from consuming email resources.

11. Gaps, Chokepoints & Missed Opportunities

🔴 Critical Gaps (Revenue Impact: High)

GAP-1: 20-day post-purchase suppression not enforced across all flows
The 20-day exclusion currently exists only in the digest script (get_recent_purchaser_ids()). The live Klaviyo flows (checkout, cart, browse) do NOT have this filter. A customer who just purchased can still receive abandoned cart emails from the flow — this damages trust and brand perception.
Fix: Add flow filter to every Klaviyo flow: Placed Order NOT in last 20 days. This is a 5-minute fix per flow in Klaviyo's flow builder. Priority: DO THIS FIRST.
GAP-2: No Klaviyo Thank You email (post-purchase)
Currently, order confirmation is handled only by Shopify's default transactional email. Klaviyo has no post-purchase flow. This means: (a) no brand-consistent post-purchase experience, (b) no lead-in to care instructions, reviews, or cross-sell, (c) no Klaviyo attribution data for post-purchase engagement.
Fix: Create a Klaviyo flow triggered by Placed Order. Email 1 = Thank You (immediate). Start simple, expand later.
GAP-3: Smart Sending may block high-intent checkout/cart emails
If Smart Sending is set to 24 hours (or any window) and a new subscriber received the Welcome email, the Checkout Flow Email #1 will be BLOCKED if they start checkout within that window. For a $750+ AOV store, this is a direct revenue leak.
Fix: (a) Set global Smart Sending to 16 hours, (b) Set Checkout #1 and Cart #1 to "Skip Smart Sending" in flow settings. Verify this setting TODAY.
GAP-4: Digest script runs manually — no automation
The daily_digest_updater.py script must be run manually before each campaign send. If forgotten, profiles have stale data and the campaign sends outdated product info (or doesn't send at all if properties are empty).
Fix: Set up a cron job (macOS launchd or crontab) to run the script at 7:00 PM every Tue and Fri. Example: 0 19 * * 2,5 cd /path/to/project && .venv/bin/python migration/daily_digest_updater.py
GAP-5: Collection page UTM bug still active
Klaviyo UTM parameters break Shopify collection page loading. Links to collection pages in ANY email (flow or campaign) will fail. This affects every email that links to a collection (e.g., "Browse our Rings" CTA → collection URL → broken page for the customer).
Fix: See diagnostic steps in the mode instructions. Test, fix, verify. Every email audit should flag this until resolved.

🟡 Chokepoints (Operational Risk)

CHOKE-1: Checkout Flow + Checkout Digest overlap
The existing live Checkout Flow may have follow-up emails beyond #1 and #2 that could overlap with the Checkout Digest campaign on Day 3. If the flow sends a 3rd email AND the digest campaign fires on the same day, the customer gets doubled.
Fix: Audit the existing Checkout Flow in Klaviyo. Count the emails. If more than 2, either (a) disable flow emails beyond #2 (let digest take over), or (b) add a conditional split in the flow that checks checkout_digest_count > 0.
CHOKE-2: Campaign vs. flow timing mismatch
The digest is campaign-based (fires on Tue/Fri schedule). A customer who checks out on Monday gets Checkout Flow #1 at +1h and #2 on Tuesday — then the Checkout Digest also fires on Tuesday. That's 2 emails on Tuesday.
Fix: The segment condition "Checkout Started > 48h ago" mitigates this (see Section 9). But consider converting the digest to a segment-triggered FLOW for per-person timing. This is the cleanest long-term solution.
CHOKE-3: Browse → Cart escalation mid-flow
When a customer is mid-way through the Browse Abandonment flow (e.g., received Browse #2 on Day 3) and adds to cart, does the Browse flow exit cleanly? Klaviyo flows with "Added to Cart" exit conditions should handle this, but needs verification.
Fix: Verify the Browse Abandonment flow has an exit condition: "Profile has Added to Cart at least once since entering this flow." Test by triggering the scenario manually.
CHOKE-4: No global frequency cap in Klaviyo settings
Without a global cap, a profile in the dual-track scenario (Checkout + Cart) receiving 5 emails/week PLUS a lifecycle campaign PLUS welcome could exceed comfortable thresholds.
Fix: In Klaviyo Settings → Email → Sending, configure a global frequency cap. Check current setting and set to max 4–5 emails/week across all sources if not already configured.

🔵 Missed Opportunities (Revenue Upside)

OPP-1: Welcome series is only 1 email
The first 21 days after subscription is the highest-engagement window. A single welcome email captures ~15% of the available brand-building and first-purchase potential. The recommended 7-email series builds trust over the full consideration cycle.
Impact: Estimated 20–35% increase in first-purchase conversion rate from welcome subscribers. At $750 AOV, even 5 additional conversions/month = $3,750/month incremental revenue.
OPP-2: No price drop / back-in-stock alerts
Customers who viewed or carted a specific item and it later drops in price or returns to stock are the highest-intent reactivation targets. These are 1-email, high-conversion opportunities.
Impact: Price drop emails typically convert at 2–5× the rate of standard browse abandonment.
OPP-3: Cart Flow email shows single item, not all cart items
The live Cart Flow Email #1 (triggered by Added to Cart) shows only the most recent item. If a customer has added 5 items, they only see 1. The digest solves this at +2 days, but there's a 2-day gap where the customer's full cart isn't represented.
Options: (a) Accept the 2-day gap (digest fills it), (b) Convert Cart Flow #1 to use cart_digest_items property for dynamic rendering (requires script to run before flow email, which may not be realistic for a 2-hour trigger).
OPP-4: No cross-sell after post-purchase suppression lifts
At Day 20, the suppression lifts but there's no planned email for re-engagement. A customer who bought a ring 3 weeks ago is prime for a complementary piece (matching band, earrings, necklace).
Impact: Targeted cross-sell at Day 20–30 can drive repeat purchases. "Complete the set" or "Pair with your [purchased item]."
OPP-5: No VIP differentiation
Profiles with $2,000+ lifetime value or $1,500+ single orders receive the same emails as $150 sterling silver buyers. VIP customers warrant: personal consultant outreach on high-value checkouts, early access to new collections, exclusive service offers.
Impact: VIP customers have the highest reactivation potential. Even small improvements in VIP retention yield outsized revenue.
OPP-6: No winback or sunset flows
Customers who haven't purchased in 180+ days have no automated re-engagement. Unengaged profiles (120+ days no opens/clicks) have no sunset flow, potentially hurting deliverability.
Impact: Winback flows for jewelry typically recover 3–8% of lapsed customers. Sunset flows protect deliverability (reduces complaints and bounces).
